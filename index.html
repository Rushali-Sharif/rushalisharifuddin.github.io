I am currently a Research Collaborator at ELITE Research Lab . Previously, I served as a Lecturer in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at United International University , Bangladesh, and as a Research Engineer at Advanced Intelligent Multidisciplinary Systems (AIMS) Lab .

Research Interests

I am interested in building visual intelligence systems that do more than recognize objects — systems that actively construct their own understanding of the world. I call this direction Autopoietic Visual Intelligence: visual models that can integrate signals from multiple modalities (vision, depth, motion, language, sound), build internal memory of experience, and continuously refine what they consider meaningful without relying on fixed human ontologies or dense human supervision.

The goal is to move from passive perception (“this matches a known label”) to self-evolving interpretation (“this is a structure I have discovered, tracked, and assigned significance to”). A key motivation is that in many real environments — underwater, clinical, field robotics, low-resource regions — we simply do not have clean labels, stable lighting, or abundant compute.

Instead of depending on large annotated datasets, these systems must learn from weak cues, temporal continuity, cross-modal agreement, and self-generated hypotheses. This involves enabling models to adapt in open, shifting conditions with minimal supervision; giving them structured memory so they can revisit and reinterpret past experience; letting them imagine counterfactuals to test their own beliefs; and pushing them to explain and reuse their internally formed concepts.

Ultimately, this asks whether an artificial visual system can develop its own semantics, its own priorities, and its own way of seeing — and whether that kind of self-constructed perception is the path to reliable, controllable AI that works where labels, power, and infrastructure are scarce.
